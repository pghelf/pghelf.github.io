About

Here is where you can learn more about me!

My name is Patricia and I am a junior in college. I have been an avid reader since I was a child. A few of my favorite genres include mystery, thriller, and historical fiction.

I will be using this site as a personal library where I will give reviews of books I have read as well as recommendations based on my reviews.



Reviews

Check out my reviews! I have rated all books on a 5-star rating scale.

Thrillers

This is where you will find reviews of the mystery books I have read.

The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides

I give this book 5/5 stars. I thoroughly enjoyed this book. I read this book in one day because I just couldn't put it down. There is a huge twist that I did not see coming and the ending was perfect. The ending perfectly tied together all the elements of the story in a way that made my jaw drop.

The Guest List by Lucy Foley

I give this book 4/5 stars. This was an amazing book. I loved the way it was written and how the chapters alternated between the present time of the story and the past parts of the story. It kept me on my toes and I felt like I was connecting clues throughout the entire book. The ending was not expected and I absolutely didn't see it coming. While I enjoyed the chapters going back and forth between the present time of the store, it also got a littel confusingat times, especially when I would come back to the story after a while of not reading it.

Verity by Colleen Hoover

I give this book 3.5/5 stars. I know there has been a lot of discussion about Colleen Hoover and after reading this book, I can see why. This is the first book I read by Colleeen Hoover. While the story was well-written, I felt like she relied too much on explicit content. The whole book felt like I was reading a crazy person's obession about their significant other and a 3rd party's attempt to try to break up a family. The book had lots of twists, but ended in a way that was somewhat expected.

Historical Fiction

The Nightingale by Kristin Hannah

This book was a story about two sisters trying to find a way to survive WWII in France. This book was an amazing tear-jerker. It was beautifully written and caused so many tears, but ended in a way that I was not expecting. It's a longer book, but it's well worth the read. Because of Kristin Hannah's wonderful writing and story-telling, she has become one of my favorite authors.

Mysteries

Where the Crawdads Sing by Delia Owens

I give this book 4.5/5 stars. I loved this book. It was wonderfully written and contained a surprise ending! I gave it 4.5 stars because I feel like the last 1-2 pages kind of ruined the story for me. I thought it was the story about a girl's triumph, and the very last pages had me left in disbelief.

Other Genres

The Girl and the Goddess: Stories and Poems of Divine Wisdom by Nikita Gill

I give this book 5/5 stars! As someone who enjoys a good book of poetry every now and then, this book was amazing. There were so many poems about growing up as a girl and learnign how to face struggles women face in the world as they age. This book had great stories and I found myself tagging so many pages regarding women empowerment.

The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo by Taylor Jenkins Reid

I give this book 5/5 stars! This was the book that got me back into reading again. It was written so well and the romance aspect of the book had me tearing up at multiple parts of the story. This was a great book and she has become one of my favorite authors.

To Be Read

Here is a list of all the books I want to read, but have not read just yet.

Thrillers

Gone Girl by Gillian Flynn

This book has been on my To Be Read list for a while, but I have not gotten around to reading it. This book is about a man's struggle to find figure out what happens to his wife after she disappears on the morning of their fifth wedding anniversary.

The Woman in Cabin 10 by Ruth Ware

This book is about a journalist who writes for a travel magazine who has been given the assignment to go on a week-long luxury cruise with only a few cabins. At first, the trip is pleasant, but throughtout the week, things become ominous as she thinks she has seen a woman being thrown overboard. All passengers remain accounted for despite the main character's attempts to convey that something, or someone, has gone wrong.

Rock Paper Scissors by Alice Feeney

This book is about a married couple that wins a weekend away in Scotland. The husband has lived with face blindness his whole life and cannot recognize his friends, family, or wife. Each year on their anniversary, they exchange traditional gifts and his wife writes a letter that she never lets him read, until now. The couple knows that the weekend away will either make or break their marriage, but one of them is lying about how they got the trip in the first place, since they did not randomly win the weekend getaway.

Mysteries

The Inheritance Games by Jennifer Lynn Barnes

This book is about a high schooler's plan to survive high school, get a scholarship, and get out. A billionaire dies and leaves the high schooler with virtually his entire fortune. The high schooler has no idea why she has been left with all this money, or even who the billionaire was. To receive the inheritance, she must move in to his house, which is filled with secret passages, where every room includes puzzles, riddles, and codes. The house is also occupied by the billionaire's family. The family is convinced the high schooler must be some kind of con artist and they try to take her down. Even though the high schooler has come into a world of wealth and privilege, there is danger at every turn and she must play the inheritance games herself to survive.

The Maid by Nina Prose

This book is about a woman who struggles with social skills and often misreads the intention of others. Her grandmother used to interpret the world for her and gave her simple rules to live by, but her grandmother died and she has had to navigate through life all on her own. She has an obsessive live for cleaning and proper etiquette so she lands a job as a hotel maid. One day, she enters the suite of a wealthy man and finds it completely in disarray. She also finds the wealthy man dead. Because of her unusual demeanor, she finds herself to be targeted by the police as a the number one suspect. Friends of the maid, that she didn't even know she had, unite to help her try to find clues as to who the real killer is before it's too late. This book is stated to be a Clue-like mystery.

Historical Fiction

Daisy Jones & The Six by Taylor Jenkins Reid

This book is about a girl named Daisy coming of age in the sixties in LA. She loves rock n' roll and by the time she is 20, her voice is starting to get noticed. A band called The Six is also getting noticed. On the eve of their first tour, the band's leading man's girlfriend finds out she is pregnant and he goes crazy while on the road. A producer realizes that Daisy and the leading man of The Six need to be put together to become successful. This book is written as an oral history of one of the biggest bands of the seventies.

The Four Winds by Kristin Hannah

This book takes place in Texas in 1934 and is about the Dust Bowl Era during the Great Depression. Millions of peopel are out of work and a drought has broken out across the Great Plains. Crops are failing, water is drying up, and the dust threatens to bury farmers as they try to keep their land and livelihood. The main character, Elsa, has to make the decision to either fight for her land or go West to California in search of a better life.

Other Genres

The Cold Vanish by Jon Billman

This book examines real-life cases of those who vanish in the wilderness without a trace, and the people who are determined to find them. This book tells the different stories of people who have gone missing and their loved one's efforts to try to find them.

Plagues and Peoples by William H. McNeill

This book is about the history of disease. It goes through the epidemics of smallpox, the bubonic plauge in China, and the typhoid epidemic in Europe.

The Indifferent Stars Above: The Harrowing Saga of the Donner Party

This book is about a 21 year old woman who sets out west from Illinois with her new husband, her parents, and her 8 siblings in 1846. 7 months after leaving Illinois, they join a party of pioneers led by George Donner. They reach the Sierra Nevada Mountains as the first heavy snows of the season closed the pass ahead of them. In early December, the 21 year old woman and 14 others set out for California on snowshows and endure almost unfathomable horros and hardships.



My Personal Favorites

Here is where you can find a list of my personal favorite books I have read.

All Genres

This list will not be broken into different genres, but each book will have an explanation of why it has become one of my personal favorites.

The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides

This book was an insane page-turner and helped me get out of my reading slump. I read this book in one day and only put it down once or twice. The entire story was very well written and it had me guessing all the way until the very end. The ending was something I was not expecting and I couldn't believe the insane twist this book had. This has become one of my personal favorites purely for the surprise ending and twist.

The Guest List by Lucy Foley

I really enjoyed how this book was written. The chapters go back and forth from present time to days, weeks, months, or even years earlier. While it could be hard to keep track of if you read a few chapters and would come back to it a few days later, I felt like the way it was laid out is what kept me guessing until the end. I also almost felt like I was trying to figure it out before the book explained what happended. This has become one of my favorite books because of the layout of how it was written and the twist was somethign I never saw coming.

The Nightingale by Kristin Hannah

I feel like there are not enough words I could use to describe how much I loved this book. While it is a longer book, it is so well written and it evoked so much emotion.
